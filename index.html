Accra, Ghana

Ghana, a country located in West Africa, has a rich history and culture. The ancient kingdom of Ghana was a powerful and prosperous empire that thrived on the trans-Saharan trade route. In the 15th century, European colonizers arrived, and the British eventually took control, naming the colony the Gold Coast. In 1957, Ghana became the first country in sub-Saharan Africa to gain independence, led by Kwame Nkrumah. Today, Ghana is a democratic country with a vibrant culture, diverse wildlife, and a strong sense of community. From the bustling streets of Accra to the ancient mosques of Larabanga, Ghana is a country with a deep history and a bright future.
